COMMON REPORTING STANDARD

OECD Common Reporting Standard

The Common Reporting Standard (CRS) is the global framework developed by the OECD for the automatic exchange of financial account information between tax authorities. Adopted by over 100 jurisdictions, CRS requires Financial Institutions — banks, brokers, investment managers, and certain insurance companies — to collect and verify the tax residency of their account holders, and to report financial account information to their local tax authority for onward exchange with other participating jurisdictions.

Financial Institution Categories
🏦 Depository Institution Accepts deposits in the ordinary course of a banking or similar business.
📂 Custodial Institution Holds financial assets for the account of others as a substantial part of its business.
📈 Investment Entity Primarily invests, reinvests, or trades in financial assets and is managed by another FI.
🛡 Specified Insurance Company Issues insurance or annuity contracts with a cash value component.

Reporting Timeline

CRS obligations follow an annual cycle. Exact reporting deadlines vary by jurisdiction — check your local implementing legislation for the precise dates applicable to you.

1
Collect

Throughout the year

Obtain self-certifications for all new accounts at opening. Apply due diligence procedures to pre-existing accounts. Monitor for changes in circumstances.

2
Review

By 31 December

Complete annual review of all High Value individual accounts (≥ USD 1,000,000). Confirm year-end account balances and income/proceeds for all reportable accounts.

3
Report

Typically by 30 June

Submit the annual CRS return to your competent authority. Covers all Reportable Accounts as at 31 December of the preceding calendar year.

4
Exchange

Typically by 30 September

Your competent authority automatically exchanges the reported information with the tax authorities of the account holders' jurisdictions of residence.

Due Diligence Thresholds

CRS due diligence requirements differ depending on whether an account is new or pre-existing, and whether it is held by an individual or entity. Balance thresholds determine the level of procedures required.

Individual Accounts
New account Self-certification required — no threshold
Pre-existing — Lower Value Balance < USD 1,000,000 — residence address review
Pre-existing — High Value Balance ≥ USD 1,000,000 — enhanced due diligence + relationship manager enquiry
Entity Accounts
New account Self-certification required — no threshold
Pre-existing — below threshold Balance ≤ USD 250,000 — no due diligence required
Pre-existing — above threshold Balance > USD 250,000 — full entity due diligence required

Controlling Persons are the natural persons who exercise control over a Passive NFE. Where a Passive NFE is a Reportable Account, or where its Controlling Persons are resident in a Reportable Jurisdiction, the following information must be reported for each relevant Controlling Person.

Per Controlling Person
Full name
Residential address
Jurisdiction(s) of tax residence
Tax Identification Number (TIN) for each jurisdiction of residence
Date and place of birth

Controlling Person information is reported alongside the entity account information — it does not replace it. Both the entity-level and Controlling Person-level fields must be included in the CRS return.

Key Definitions

Select a term to expand its definition under the OECD Common Reporting Standard.

Reporting Financial Institution (RFI)
A Financial Institution that is resident in a Reportable Jurisdiction and is not a Non-Reporting Financial Institution. RFIs are obligated to perform due diligence on their account holders and report Reportable Accounts to their competent authority annually.
Non-Reporting Financial Institution
A Financial Institution that is exempt from CRS reporting obligations. Examples include governmental entities, international organisations, central banks, broad participation retirement funds, and certain low-risk collective investment vehicles. The full list is defined in each jurisdiction's implementing legislation.
Account Holder
The person listed or identified as the holder of a Financial Account by the Financial Institution that maintains the account. A person holding a Financial Account for the benefit of another person as agent, custodian, nominee, signatory, or legal guardian is not treated as the Account Holder — the beneficiary is.
Reportable Account
A Financial Account that is held by one or more Reportable Persons, or by a Passive NFE with one or more Controlling Persons who are Reportable Persons, and has been identified as such under the applicable due diligence procedures.
Self-Certification
A declaration provided directly by an account holder (or Controlling Person) to a Reporting Financial Institution confirming their jurisdiction(s) of tax residence and Tax Identification Number(s). Under CRS, a self-certification is required for all new accounts at the time of opening. The RFI must verify the reasonableness of the self-certification based on information collected during account opening.
Pre-existing Account
A Financial Account maintained by a Reporting Financial Institution as of the date immediately before CRS implementation in the relevant jurisdiction. The due diligence procedures and timelines applicable to pre-existing accounts differ from those for new accounts, and balance thresholds apply for certain account types.
Non-Financial Entity (NFE)
Any entity that is not a Financial Institution. NFEs are classified as either Active or Passive, which determines the due diligence obligations applicable when an NFE holds an account at a Reporting Financial Institution.
Active NFE
An NFE where less than 50% of its gross income for the preceding calendar year is passive income (e.g. dividends, interest, royalties) AND less than 50% of the assets held during the preceding year are assets that produce or are held for the production of passive income. Active NFEs are not subject to Controlling Person identification requirements.
Passive NFE
Any NFE that is not an Active NFE — i.e. 50% or more of its gross income is passive, or 50% or more of its assets produce or are held for passive income. For Passive NFEs, the Reporting FI must identify the Controlling Persons and determine whether any are Reportable Persons. Holding companies, shell companies, and investment vehicles typically qualify as Passive NFEs.
Controlling Person
The natural persons who exercise control over an entity. For companies: typically those holding more than 25% of shares or voting rights. For trusts: the settlor(s), trustee(s), protector(s), beneficiaries or class of beneficiaries, and any other natural person exercising ultimate effective control. For other legal arrangements: persons in equivalent positions. Where no natural person is identified as a Controlling Person, the senior managing official of the entity is treated as the Controlling Person.
Indicia of Residence
Indicators found in account records suggesting that an account holder may be tax resident in a Reportable Jurisdiction. Indicia include: a Reportable Jurisdiction address or residence address; a Reportable Jurisdiction telephone number; standing instructions to transfer funds to an account in a Reportable Jurisdiction; a currently effective power of attorney granted to a person with a Reportable Jurisdiction address; and a hold mail instruction or in-care-of address in a Reportable Jurisdiction.
Change in Circumstances
Any change that results in the addition of information relevant to an account holder's status or that is otherwise inconsistent with the account holder's status on file. Examples include a change of address, a new telephone number in a different country, a new power of attorney, or new standing instructions to transfer funds. Upon detecting a change in circumstances, the RFI must obtain a new self-certification or other documentation.
