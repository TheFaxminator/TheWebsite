DIGITAL PLATFORM INFORMATION

OECD Model Rules for Digital Platforms

The OECD Model Rules for Reporting by Platform Operators (MRDP) require digital platforms to collect, verify, and report information about sellers earning income through their platforms. Now enacted across many jurisdictions — including EU member states under DAC7 — the rules ensure sellers pay the correct tax on platform income. Use the decision tools below to find out whether the rules apply to your platform and which sellers must be reported.

The Four Relevant Activities
🏠
Rental of Immovable Property Short-term and long-term rentals of residential and commercial property, including parking spaces.
👤
Personal Services Time- or task-based work performed by an individual, such as freelance, gig, or on-demand services.
📦

Reporting Timeline

Platform operators follow a four-phase annual cycle, from collecting seller data throughout the year through to automatic exchange of information between tax authorities.

1
Collect

Throughout the year

Gather seller identity, tax residency, and financial account information. Collect all required information from sellers at the point of registration.

2
Verify

Ongoing — complete by 31 Dec

Validate seller information via plausibility checks, TIN validation, and registry lookups. All due diligence must be complete before year-end.

3
Report

31 January (following year)

Submit the annual report to your competent authority covering all reportable sellers from the preceding calendar year (1 Jan – 31 Dec).

4
Exchange

By 28/29 February

Your tax authority automatically exchanges the reported data with the tax authorities of each seller's jurisdiction of residence.

Seller Verification Guidelines

Collecting seller information is only the first step. The OECD Model Rules require platform operators to verify that information is accurate and reliable. The following steps reflect the due diligence procedures under the rules.

1

Collect Seller Information at Registration

At the point of registration, collect the required information from each seller — name, address, jurisdiction(s) of tax residence, and TIN — before the seller's first transaction. Retain all collected information as part of your due diligence records.

2

Plausibility Check

Compare the information provided by the seller against other data you hold — bank account country, registered address, IP address, or property location. If anything appears inconsistent, request clarification or corrected information from the seller before treating it as reliable.

3

TIN Format Validation

Verify that the TIN matches the correct format for the seller's stated jurisdiction — an incorrect format is a strong signal the information provided may be unreliable. Use the Global TIN Validator on this platform for supported jurisdictions.

4

Entity Registry Verification

For entity sellers, confirm legal existence via publicly available business registries and check that name, registration number, and address match the information provided by the seller. Examples: Brønnøysundregistrene (Norway), Bolagsverket (Sweden), Companies House (UK), Handelsregister (Germany).

5

Document-Based Verification

Where the plausibility check raises concerns or the seller is high-volume, request supporting documents. Individuals: government-issued photo ID and proof of address (within 3 years). Entities: certificate of incorporation or tax registration certificate.

6

Annual Review & Update

Review seller information annually, or whenever circumstances suggest it may have changed, and collect updated information from the seller where needed. Sellers who fail to provide required information should be reported with available data and, where your jurisdiction requires it, have their account restricted.

Key Definitions

Select a term to expand its definition under the OECD Model Rules.

Platform
Any software, including a website or part of one, and applications, including mobile applications, accessible by users and allowing Sellers to be connected to other users for the purpose of carrying out a Relevant Activity. Software used exclusively for processing payments or for listing and advertising a Relevant Activity (without facilitating the underlying transaction) does not qualify as a Platform.
Platform Operator
An entity that contracts with Sellers to make all or part of a Platform available to such Sellers. A Platform Operator may itself be a Seller on its own Platform — for example, a marketplace that also sells its own products alongside third-party sellers.
Reportable Platform Operator
A Platform Operator that is (a) resident for tax purposes in a Reportable Jurisdiction; (b) incorporated under the laws of a Reportable Jurisdiction; or (c) has its place of effective management in a Reportable Jurisdiction. Excluded Platform Operators are not Reportable Platform Operators.
Excluded Platform Operator
A Platform Operator that has demonstrated to the relevant competent authority that its entire business model is such that it does not have Reportable Sellers — for example, a platform that connects only large businesses that are themselves excluded from the rules as Governmental Entities or Publicly Traded Corporations.
Seller
A user of the Platform — whether an individual or an entity — who is registered at any point during the reporting period and who carries out a Relevant Activity for Consideration. A Seller does not have to be resident in a Reportable Jurisdiction to be a Seller; residency determines whether they are a Reportable Seller.
Active Seller
A Seller who, during the reporting period, has performed at least one Relevant Activity or has received Consideration for a Relevant Activity facilitated through the Platform. Sellers with zero transactions and zero payments in the period are not Active Sellers and fall outside the scope of reporting requirements.
Reportable Seller
An Active Seller who is not an Excluded Seller and who is resident for tax purposes in a Reportable Jurisdiction, or who rents out immovable property located in a Reportable Jurisdiction. Each Reportable Seller must be individually identified and reported to the competent authority in the Platform Operator's jurisdiction.
Excluded Seller
A Seller that falls into one of three categories: (a) a Governmental Entity; (b) an entity whose stock is regularly traded on an established securities market, or a Related Entity of such a corporation; or (c) an entity for which the Platform facilitated more than 2,000 property rental transactions during the reporting period. Excluded Sellers are exempt from reporting, but the basis for exclusion should be documented.
Consideration
Any compensation, in any form, paid or credited to a Seller in connection with a Relevant Activity — net of fees, commissions, or taxes withheld or charged by the Platform Operator. Consideration is reported in the currency in which it was paid, broken down per quarter. For property rental, it is reported per property address.
De Minimis Threshold (Goods Sellers)
For Sellers whose Relevant Activity consists exclusively of the sale of goods: no reporting is required if the Seller completed fewer than 30 transactions AND received less than EUR 2,000 in aggregate Consideration during the reporting period. Both thresholds must be met simultaneously. If either is exceeded, full reporting applies.
